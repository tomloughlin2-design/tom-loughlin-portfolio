# Portfolio Site Plan

## Overview
This document outlines the structure and content strategy for a personal portfolio site showcasing my work as a Designer and Product Leader, with emphasis on my experience at Tripadvisor and Viator.

The goal is to position myself not only as a design leader, but as a product leader who thinks in systems and drives organizational impact.

---

## Core Positioning
This portfolio should communicate:

- Senior design and product leadership experience
- Ability to scale teams, systems, and product experiences
- Deep experience in large-scale consumer marketplace and product environments
- Strong product thinking beyond UI design
- Systems thinking and strategic product leadership

---

## Site Structure

### 1. Home / Landing Page
Purpose: Immediate clarity of who I am and what I do.

Content:
- Short positioning statement (1–2 sentences)
- 3 pillars of expertise:
  - Product & Design Leadership
  - Marketplace / Consumer Product Strategy
  - Systems thinking and product scaling
- Featured case studies (2–4 max)
- Links to Resume and LinkedIn

---

### 2. Case Studies (Core Section)
Purpose: Demonstrate impact, decision-making, and leadership.

Each case study should include:

- Context: What problem existed?
- My role: Scope, leadership level, team influence
- What I changed: Decisions, strategy, execution
- Impact: Outcomes (quantitative or directional)
- Learnings: What I took away

Types of stories to include:
- Product leadership initiatives (e.g., app-first strategy at Viator)
- Design leadership (scaling teams, UX consistency)
- Cross-functional transformation work
- Marketplace experience improvements (Viator/Tripadvisor)

---

### 3. Leadership Philosophy
Purpose: Show how I think as a senior leader.

Include principles such as:

- Clarity over consensus
- Optimize for long-term LTV, not short-term metrics
- Start with user friction, not features
- Systems thinking over isolated design decisions
- Teams should own outcomes, not outputs
- I have led teams through both rapid scaling and organizational restructuring, maintaining clarity, trust, and product focus through both growth and constraint

---

### 4. Design System / Scaling Systems Case Study
Purpose: Demonstrate ability to scale design across an organization.

Positioning:
Instead of "design system documentation", frame as:

"Scaling Product Consistency Across a Global Marketplace"

Include:
- Problem (fragmented UX, inconsistent patterns across teams)
- Leadership role (alignment across design, product, and engineering)
- System design approach (standards, governance, adoption model)
- Impact (speed of delivery, consistency, reduced friction, improved UX coherence)

Important:
Focus on organizational and product impact, not component library details.

---

### 5. About Page
Purpose: Tell a narrative career story.

Include:
- Career progression (designer → product leader)
- Why I focus on large-scale consumer marketplaces
- What I care about in product and leadership today
- Personal tone (less corporate, more narrative)

---

### 6. Artifacts / Work Samples (Optional but valuable)
Include:
- Strategy documents (sanitized)
- UX flows and journey maps
- Product thinking diagrams
- Org/system design artifacts

Focus on thinking, not polish.

---

### 7. Testimonials (Optional)
Only include if:
- Specific
- Outcome-driven
- Credible from peers or leadership

Avoid generic praise that lacks context.

---

### 8. Resume + Contact
Simple and direct:
- Download resume
- LinkedIn link
- Email contact

---

## Tone & Visual Direction
- Minimal, modern, confident
- Not overly flashy or animation-heavy
- Focus on clarity and thinking over decoration
- Executive-level positioning, not junior portfolio style

---

## Key Differentiator
This portfolio is not just a showcase of work.

It is a demonstration of:
- Leadership thinking at scale
- Product strategy ability
- System-level design impact
- Ability to lead through both growth and organizational change

---

## Success Criteria
The site should make it clear that I am suited for:
- Senior Director / VP Product Design roles
- Product leadership roles in large-scale consumer marketplaces
- Organizations transitioning toward more system-driven and scalable product development
